## Annotation

This academic article by Jasper Verstappen investigates the **token container model** to determine if current **European private law** can accommodate the transfer of property rights via blockchain technology. The author explores the tension between **decentralised finance** innovations and traditional legal requirements, such as the necessity for **physical delivery** or formal registration in property transfers. By analysing various national frameworks, the research identifies **fundamental legal obstacles**, including rigid documentation standards and the inability of digital tokens to mirror **retroactive legal effects**, that prevent tokens from functioning as valid instruments for transferring absolute rights. Ultimately, the text serves as a critical evaluation of how **property and contract law** must evolve to provide a stable foundation for the growing **digital asset economy**.
