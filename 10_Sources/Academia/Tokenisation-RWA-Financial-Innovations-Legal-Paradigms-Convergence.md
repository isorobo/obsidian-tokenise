## Annotation

The source explores the technological shift from legacy database systems to **tokenised asset infrastructures**, where property rights and financial logic are embedded directly into programmable digital objects. It examines how this transition addresses inefficiencies like settlement delays and high costs through **shared ledgers and atomic settlement**, while navigating complex **legal paradigms** such as the dual ledger problem in real estate and regional property law variations. The text highlights diverse case studies, ranging from **fractionalised property and agricultural commodities** to the environmental transparency of **tokenised carbon credits**, all supported by a modern monetary stack of stablecoins and digital currencies. Furthermore, it outlines the emerging **agentic AI economy** where autonomous software conducts machine-to-machine transactions, alongside institutional efforts to overcome the tokenisation ceiling by moving beyond isolated private networks toward global, interoperable standards. Underpinning the entire discourse is a focus on shifting systemic risk from traditional institutions to the **correctness and resilience of code**, aiming for a more transparent and liquid global economy.
