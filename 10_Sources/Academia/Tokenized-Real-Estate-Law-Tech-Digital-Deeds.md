## Annotation

This legal essay investigates the feasibility of **real estate tokenisation**, a process where digital assets like NFTs are used to represent and transfer ownership of physical property. The authors identify a **stark disconnect** between technological potential and current **American property law**, arguing that tokenisation is a **legal construct** rather than a purely technological one. While modern statutes like the **E-Sign Act and UETA** allow for electronic records to serve as deeds, the existing **title assurance system** and the **Statute of Frauds** prevent tokens from functioning as bearer instruments that transfer title through simple possession. Ultimately, the text concludes that while blockchain can improve **transactional efficiency**, it cannot bypass the **dual ledger problem** created by the mandatory requirement to maintain traditional county land records to protect against competing claims.
