## Annotation

The provided source outlines a vision for the **Agent Economy**, a digital landscape where AI systems transition from simple tools to **autonomous economic peers** capable of independent financial action. This transformation is made possible by a **five-layer blockchain architecture** that provides agents with **decentralised identities**, financial autonomy through smart contracts, and the ability to conduct **machine-to-machine micropayments** without human intermediaries. By decoupling AI from human-centric legal and banking constraints, the framework establishes a foundation for the **Internet of Agents**, where machines manage their own resources and participate in collective governance via **Agentic DAOs**. Despite this potential, the text highlights critical research hurdles such as **verification through zero-knowledge proofs**, legal liability for autonomous actions, and the essential need to ensure these systems support **human flourishing** rather than displacing it.
