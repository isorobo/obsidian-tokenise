## Annotation

This BIS Bulletin provides a comprehensive primer on the **tokenisation of traditional assets**, exploring how converting claims into digital tokens on a **common programmable platform** can modernise the global financial system. The text outlines a structural transition from traditional, siloed ledgers to a unified architecture where assets and the rules governing them are integrated into a single **executable object** via smart contracts. By examining the **tokenisation continuum**, the authors argue that while digitising simple assets is easier, the most significant economic gains, such as **atomic settlement** and improved liquidity, come from overcoming the complex legal and technical hurdles of more difficult use cases. Ultimately, the paper serves to explain how this technological shift could eliminate costly intermediaries and enable **composability**, allowing for automated, multi-step transactions that were previously impossible due to information gaps or counterparty risks.
