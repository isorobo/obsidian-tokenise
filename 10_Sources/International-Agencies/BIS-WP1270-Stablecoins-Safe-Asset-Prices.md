# Stablecoins and safe asset prices

## Citation

Ahmed, Rashad, and Iñaki Aldasoro. "Stablecoins and safe asset prices". BIS Working Papers No 1270, 28 May 2025. https://www.bis.org/publ/work1270.htm.

## One-line summary

Dollar-backed stablecoin inflows reduce short-term US Treasury bill yields by 2.5 to 3.5 basis points per two-standard-deviation shock, with larger effects during bill scarcity.

## Key claims

- As of March 2025, dollar-backed stablecoins held over $200 billion in assets, exceeding major foreign investors' combined short-term US securities holdings.
- Stablecoin issuers purchased $40 billion in Treasury bills during 2024, making them a material marginal buyer in short-duration safe asset markets.
- A two-standard-deviation stablecoin inflow lowers three-month Treasury bill yields by 2.5 to 3.5 basis points, with minimal spillover to longer maturities.
- The yield effect is state-dependent: it rises to 5 to 8 basis points during bill scarcity periods, measured by reverse repo facility growth and debt ceiling standoffs.
- Tether (USDT) and Circle (USDC) account for the majority of observed market effects; the sector remains highly concentrated.

## Significance

- Provides the first instrumental-variable estimate of stablecoin demand on sovereign safe asset pricing, anchoring the empirical debate on systemic relevance.
- Demonstrates that stablecoin reserve composition creates a structural link between crypto market cycles and short-duration sovereign debt markets.
- The state-dependent effect during bill scarcity is directly relevant to regulatory debates on reserve transparency and liquidity requirements for stablecoin issuers.
- Grounds the wiki's stablecoin-monetary-policy axis with primary empirical evidence from a BIS-authored paper.
