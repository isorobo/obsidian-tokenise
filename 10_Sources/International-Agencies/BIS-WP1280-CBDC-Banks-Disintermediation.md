# CBDC and banks: disintermediating fast and slow

## Citation

Bidder, Rhys, Timothy Jackson, and Matthias Rottner. "CBDC and banks: disintermediating fast and slow". BIS Working Papers No 1280, 8 July 2025. https://www.bis.org/publ/work1280.htm.

## One-line summary

A retail CBDC with no holding limits would reduce financial stability because it provides a convenient, scalable exit from bank deposits during crises, a risk holding limits can mitigate.

## Key claims

- German household survey data show that households would hold CBDC as a deposit substitute in normal times, reducing bank funding.
- CBDC's "storage-at-scale" property makes it structurally more attractive during financial stress than cash, amplifying the speed of bank runs.
- The dual disintermediation risk operates on two timescales: slow erosion of deposit funding in calm periods, and fast run dynamics in stress.
- Holding limits on CBDC reduce systemic fragility while preserving some of CBDC's monetary-policy transmission benefits.
- The paper's macroeconomic model incorporates systemic bank runs, distinguishing it from models that treat CBDC substitution as a steady-state phenomenon.
