# Competing digital monies

## Citation

Frost, Jon, Jean-Charles Rochet, Hyun Song Shin, and Marianne Verdier. "Competing digital monies". BIS Working Papers No 1301, 11 November 2025. https://www.bis.org/publ/work1301.htm.

## One-line summary

Two-sided market theory shows that fast payment systems and CBDCs both achieve interoperability among competing digital monies, improving financial inclusion more than unregulated market competition.

## Key claims

- Payment systems operating as isolated "walled gardens" produce inefficiently low financial inclusion and trading volumes.
- Fast retail payment systems that mandate interoperability eliminate financial exclusion, at the cost of some disintermediation of traditional banks.
- CBDCs that function as digital cash produce outcomes equivalent to fast payment systems by ensuring different payment instruments remain interoperable.
- Both policy approaches dominate unregulated competition among bank deposits, platform tokens, and CBDCs on welfare grounds.
- The model uses two-sided market theory to formalise why network effects in payments require public intervention or coordination.

## Significance

- Provides the formal theoretical basis for the BIS's preference for public digital payment infrastructure over private platform tokens.
- The interoperability result is directly relevant to the wiki's analysis of CBDC design choices and the MiCA regulatory treatment of e-money tokens.
- Connects the stablecoin-vs-CBDC debate to established two-sided market economics, grounding policy advocacy in industrial organisation theory.
- Authored by Hyun Song Shin (BIS Chief Economist), giving the paper outsized influence on BIS institutional positions.
