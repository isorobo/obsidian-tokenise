# When bricks meet bytes: does tokenisation fill gaps in traditional real estate markets?

## Citation

Cornelli, Giulio. "When bricks meet bytes: does tokenisation fill gaps in traditional real estate markets?". BIS Working Papers No 1311, 28 November 2025. https://www.bis.org/publ/work1311.htm.

## One-line summary

Empirical analysis of US tokenised real estate platforms (2019-2025) finds tokenisation targets underserved low-price, low-liquidity markets and delivers a 35% liquidity increase after local shocks, but platform buyback mechanisms introduce insolvency risk.

## Key claims

- Tokenised real estate properties emerge primarily in areas with lower property prices, weaker demand, and less conventional market liquidity.
- Adoption is higher in regions with limited access to traditional credit, suggesting tokenisation bridges financing gaps for retail investors.
- Following exogenous shocks such as natural disasters, trading in tokenised properties increases approximately 35% over the two days after the event.
- Liquidity advantages depend on platform buyback mechanisms, which correlate with higher insolvency risk for the platform operator.
- The paper draws on data from multiple US platforms across 2019 to 2025, providing the first systematic cross-platform empirical study of real estate tokenisation.

## Significance

- Delivers the first BIS-authored cross-platform empirical study on real estate tokenisation, establishing an authoritative benchmark for the wiki's real-estate domain.
- The credit-access finding is load-bearing for arguments that tokenisation democratises investment access rather than merely replicating existing products on a blockchain.
- The liquidity-shock result provides empirical grounding for the claim that tokenised assets outperform illiquid traditional alternatives during stress events.
- The insolvency risk of buyback mechanisms flags a structural vulnerability relevant to regulatory treatment of tokenised real estate platforms.
