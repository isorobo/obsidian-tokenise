# Tokenomics and blockchain fragmentation

## Citation

Shin, Hyun Song. "Tokenomics and blockchain fragmentation". BIS Working Papers No 1335, 10 March 2026. https://www.bis.org/publ/work1335.htm.

## One-line summary

A global-games model of blockchain validator incentives shows that decentralised consensus irreducibly fragments the monetary landscape, undermining the network effects that money requires.

## Key claims

- Decentralised consensus carries an irreducible cost: the more secure the blockchain, the higher the validator rewards required and the greater the congestion costs borne by users.
- Competing blockchains proliferate because less stringent chains emerge to serve users priced out of more secure incumbent chains.
- Fragmentation structurally undermines the network effects that give money its value as a coordination mechanism.
- Stablecoins cannot resolve blockchain fragmentation because they inherit it from their underlying infrastructure.
- The model uses global games theory to formalise validator participation decisions and their systemic monetary consequences.

## Significance

- Provides the BIS Chief Economist's formal model for why permissionless blockchain architectures are structurally incompatible with money's network-effect requirements.
- The irreducible-consensus-cost result is the theoretical counterpoint to industry claims that tokenised money on public chains can achieve monetary singleness.
- Directly relevant to the wiki's unified-ledger thesis: the fragmentation argument supports the BIS position that tokenised finance requires permissioned or hybrid infrastructure.
- Authored by Hyun Song Shin alone, giving this paper the character of a BIS institutional position statement on blockchain monetary economics.
