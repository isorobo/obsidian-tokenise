## Annotation

This IMF Note explores how **tokenisation**, or the use of programmable ledgers to represent financial claims, is driving a **structural shift in global financial architecture**. The text examines the transition from simple digitisation to a system where **settlement, liquidity management, and risk** are embedded directly into code through smart contracts and shared infrastructures. While this evolution offers significant efficiency gains like **atomic settlement**, it also introduces new vulnerabilities such as increased **procyclicality, algorithmic risks, and jurisdictional fragmentation**. Ultimately, the author argues that the stability of this new era depends on **anchoring digital finance in public trust** through clear legal frameworks, international cooperation, and safe settlement assets like central bank money.
