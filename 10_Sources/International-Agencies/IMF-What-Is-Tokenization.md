## Annotation

This article explores how **tokenisation** aims to modernise the financial system by creating **programmable digital representations** of assets on shared ledgers. By integrating **code and automated execution**, this technology seeks to eliminate the delays and expenses caused by traditional **financial intermediaries** and slow settlement processes. While these innovations offer greater **market efficiency and competition**, the text warns of significant risks, including **increased volatility, systemic complexity**, and the potential for rapid, automated financial contagion. Ultimately, the author suggests that while digital tokens represent a natural evolution of money, their implementation requires **careful regulation and interoperability** to ensure global economic stability.
