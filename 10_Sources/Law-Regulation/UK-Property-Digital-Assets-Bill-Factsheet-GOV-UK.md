## Annotation

The Property (Digital Assets Etc.) Bill is a legislative initiative designed to modernise the legal landscape by formally recognising **digital assets**, such as crypto-tokens, as a distinct form of **personal property**. Historically, the law only acknowledged items that could be physically held or claimed through legal action, but this bill creates a **third category of property rights** to accommodate intangible technology. By clarifying these legal protections, the government aims to provide **greater certainty for owners** regarding theft, inheritance, and business transactions while allowing courts the flexibility to evolve with future innovations. Ultimately, this factsheet explains how the bill secures the position of England, Wales, and Northern Ireland as **attractive jurisdictions** for the global digital economy.
