## Annotation

The Property (Digital Assets etc) Act 2025 marks a historic shift in British law by establishing a **third category of personal property** specifically for digital holdings like crypto-tokens and NFTs. Because these assets are neither physical objects nor simple legal claims, this legislation provides the **legal clarity** necessary to protect owners rights within a modern economy. While the Act formally recognises these items as property, it intentionally utilises **flexible drafting** to allow the judiciary to define specific boundaries as technology evolves. Ultimately, this statutory change aims to maintain the UK position as a global leader in finance by reducing **commercial uncertainty** and accommodating the unique characteristics of intangible digital value.
