## Annotation

This article explores the intersection of **voluntary carbon markets and blockchain technology**, specifically focusing on the process of **tokenisation** to enhance market efficiency. While these markets allow entities to purchase offsets non-compulsorily, they often struggle with issues regarding **transparency, liquidity, and the verification** of actual emission reductions. The text details how initiatives like KlimaDAO attempted to sweep the market of low-quality credits using decentralised finance, a move that ultimately highlighted systemic flaws such as the **circulation of phantom credits** that lack genuine environmental impact. Consequently, the narrative emphasises that for digital tokens to successfully mature, the industry must establish **rigorous standards for measurement and permanence** to ensure that blockchain applications provide real solutions rather than merely scaling existing inaccuracies.
