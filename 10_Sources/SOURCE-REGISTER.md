## 1. International Agencies and Standard-Setters

The standard-setters define the conceptual vocabulary — *tokenisation*, *unified ledger*, *programmable money*, *asset-referenced token* — and shape the regulatory templates that national authorities import.

### 1.1 Bank for International Settlements (BIS)

- **Carstens, A., and Nilekani, N. (2024).** "Finternet: the financial system for the future." *BIS Working Paper No. 1178.*
  Proposes a multi-network, user-centric financial system built on tokenisation and unified ledgers. The foundational vision paper for the BIS programme on tokenised finance.
  https://www.bis.org/publ/work1178.htm

- **BIS (2023).** *Blueprint for the future monetary system: improving the old, enabling the new.* Annual Economic Report 2023, Chapter III.
  Sets out the unified ledger thesis: tokenised central bank money, tokenised commercial bank deposits, and tokenised assets settling on a common programmable platform.
  https://www.bis.org/publ/arpdf/ar2023e3.htm

- **BIS Innovation Hub (2024–2026).** *Project Agorá.*
  Public-private experiment with seven central banks (Federal Reserve NY, Bank of England, ECB via Banque de France, Bank of Japan, Bank of Korea, Bank of Mexico, Swiss National Bank) and over 40 commercial banks testing tokenised commercial bank deposits settling on a tokenised wholesale CBDC. Prototype phase reports expected first half of 2026.
  https://www.bis.org/about/bisih/topics/fmis/agora.htm

- **BIS press release (24 June 2025).** "Next-generation monetary and financial system takes shape, based on a tokenised unified ledger."
  Consolidates the BIS position that tokenisation, properly architected, succeeds the legacy correspondent-banking model.
  https://www.bis.org/press/p250624.htm

- **BIS (2024–2025).** *The tokenisation continuum.* BIS Bulletin / Working Paper.
  Frames tokenisation not as a binary state but as a **spectrum** running from registry-anchored claims through partially programmable wrappers to fully on-chain, smart-contract-native assets. Useful taxonomy for positioning any specific deal or pilot. [NLM `80293ddc`]

- **Aldasoro, I., Cornelli, G., Frost, J., Gambacorta, L., and Shreeti, V. (2025).** "Stablecoins and safe asset prices." *BIS Working Paper.*
  Quantifies the spill-over from stablecoin issuance into short-dated US Treasury yields. Connects the stablecoin sub-topic to the broader RWA market through reserve-asset demand.
  Domain knowledge — confirm DOI when retrieving.

- **Auer, R., Frost, J., and others (2022–2024).** BIS papers on CBDC, tokenisation and DeFi.
  The BIS body of work on the architectural choices facing public-money tokenisation.

### 1.2 International Monetary Fund (IMF)

- **IMF (2025).** "Tokenization and Financial Market Inefficiencies." *IMF FinTech Notes Vol. 2025, Issue 001.*
  Analyses where tokenisation reduces friction (collateral mobility, intraday settlement) and where it does not (price discovery in illiquid markets). [NLM `6ffc39f6`]

- **IMF (April 2026).** *Tokenized Finance.* IMF Notes No. 26/01.
  The most recent IMF synthesis. Treats tokenisation as a stack — money, assets, infrastructure — and analyses systemic-risk transmission across the layers. Headline 2026 IMF reference. [NLM `278bc97d`]

- **IMF.** *What Is Tokenization?* Plain-language explainer.
  Useful as an onboarding source for new readers. [NLM `993dcf87`]

- **Adrian, T., and Mancini-Griffoli, T. (2021, ongoing).** IMF papers on digital money, stablecoins, and the international monetary system.
  Adrian's body of work is the leading IMF voice on the monetary-policy implications of stablecoin scaling.

### 1.3 Financial Stability Board (FSB)

- **FSB (2023, 2024).** *High-level Recommendations for the Regulation, Supervision and Oversight of Crypto-Asset Activities and Markets;* parallel recommendations on *global stablecoin arrangements.*
  The G20-mandated baseline that national supervisors are now operationalising.
  https://www.fsb.org/work-of-the-fsb/financial-innovation-and-structural-change/crypto-assets-and-global-stablecoins/

### 1.4 IOSCO

- **IOSCO (2022).** *Decentralised Finance Report.* And **(2023)** *Policy Recommendations for Crypto and Digital Asset Markets.*
  Securities-regulator consensus on how existing market-conduct standards apply to tokenised instruments.
  https://www.iosco.org/

### 1.5 OECD

- **OECD (2020).** *The Tokenisation of Assets and Potential Implications for Financial Markets.* OECD Blockchain Policy Series.
  Early but still cited; the first major intergovernmental analysis. Useful for definitional baseline.
  https://www.oecd.org/finance/The-Tokenisation-of-Assets-and-Potential-Implications-for-Financial-Markets.htm

- **OECD (2024).** *Generative AI in Finance.*
  Connects the agentic-AI sub-topic to financial supervision.

### 1.6 World Bank

- **World Bank (annual).** *State and Trends of Carbon Pricing.*
  Authoritative carbon-market data. Establishes the scale problem that tokenisation proponents address.
  https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/

### 1.7 World Economic Forum

- **WEF (2023–2025).** *Digital Assets, Bretton Woods 2.0, and Tokenisation* papers (multiple).
  Useful for stakeholder mapping across public and private sectors.

### 1.8 UNIDROIT

- **UNIDROIT (2023).** *Principles on Digital Assets and Private Law.* Adopted by the Governing Council at its 102nd session (10–12 May 2023); published 4 October 2023.
  Nineteen principles addressing control, transfer, custody, secured transactions, insolvency, and choice of law. The leading international private-law instrument for tokenised assets. Required reading for the law strand.
  https://www.unidroit.org/work-in-progress/digital-assets-and-private-law/

- **HCCH–UNIDROIT (ongoing).** *Joint Project on Digital Assets and Tokens.*
  Examines private-international-law questions for cross-border tokenisation.
  https://assets.hcch.net/docs/efce41ca-1c8c-4d61-9d8b-9348df9303a0.pdf

## 2. Central Banks and Supervisors

### 2.1 Monetary Authority of Singapore — Project Guardian

- **MAS (2022–ongoing).** *Project Guardian.*
  Cross-border sandbox with more than 40 participants (DBS, JPMorgan, UBS, Apollo, Franklin Templeton, Ant Group, OCBC, Hamilton Lane). Live use cases in tokenised funds, fixed income, and FX. Pilot of tokenised fund subscriptions and redemptions settled via Swift completed in 2025.
  https://www.mas.gov.sg/schemes-and-initiatives/project-guardian

- **MAS (November 2025).** *Project Guardian — Operationalising Tokenised Funds: Operational Guide.*
  Shared framework for fund managers, custodians, and service providers covering governance, NAV calculation, investor onboarding, and compliance for tokenised funds.
  https://www.mas.gov.sg/-/media/mas-media-library/development/fintech/guardian/project-guardian-operationalising-tokenised-funds.pdf

### 2.2 Hong Kong Monetary Authority — Project Ensemble

- **HKMA (March 2024 –).** *Project Ensemble.*
  Tokenised-deposit settlement architecture connecting commercial-bank tokenised money to the wholesale CBDC e-HKD.

- **HKMA (13 November 2025).** *EnsembleTX — pilot phase announcement.*
  Real-value transactions in tokenised deposits and digital assets. Initial focus on tokenised money-market-fund transactions and intraday liquidity management. Participants include Standard Chartered, HSBC, Bank of China (Hong Kong), BlackRock and Franklin Templeton. Settlement initially via HKD RTGS, with planned upgrade to 24/7 tokenised central-bank-money settlement.
  https://www.hkma.gov.hk/eng/news-and-media/press-releases/2025/11/20251113-3/

- **HSBC (November 2025).** First live cross-bank tokenised-deposit transaction in EnsembleTX.
  https://www.about.hsbc.com.hk/news-and-media/hsbc-completes-first-live-cross-bank-transaction-in-ensembletx

### 2.3 Other central-bank work

- **Banque de France.** Wholesale CBDC and DLT settlement experiments (Venus, Jura, Mariana). Foundational pilots for euro-area tokenised settlement.
- **Bank of England (2023).** *Discussion Paper on the regulatory regime for systemic payment systems using stablecoins and related service providers.*
- **Federal Reserve Bank of New York.** Project Cedar (FX tokenisation) and partnership in Project Agorá.
- **European Central Bank (2024–2026).** Wholesale DLT settlement trial programme.
- **Federal Reserve Board.** *Tokenization: Overview and Financial Stability Implications.* Staff working paper.
  The Federal Reserve's most-cited contribution. Maps tokenisation onto traditional financial-stability frameworks (run risk, interconnectedness, settlement risk). Sober counterweight to the industry-bullish reports in section 3. [NLM `64b66422`]

## 4. Academia

### 4.1 Foundational economic theory

- **Catalini, C., and Gans, J.S. (2016, updated 2020).** "Some Simple Economics of the Blockchain." *MIT Sloan / NBER.*
  Establishes the cost-of-verification and cost-of-networking framework.

- **Cong, L.W., and He, Z. (2019).** "Blockchain Disruption and Smart Contracts." *Review of Financial Studies* 32(5): 1754–1797.
  Smart-contract economic analysis foundational to RWA design.

- **Yermack, D. (2017).** "Corporate Governance and Blockchains." *Review of Finance* 21(1): 7–31.
  Property-rights and registry implications of distributed ledgers.

### 4.2 RWA-specific surveys

- **Xia, N., Zhao, X., Yang, Y., Li, Y., and Li, Y. (2025).** "Exploration on Real World Assets (RWAs) & Tokenization." *arXiv:2503.01111* (Columbia University).
  The most comprehensive academic survey to date. Notes tokenised treasury sector growth from USD 100 million to USD 1.3 billion between January and April 2024 (since extended).
  https://arxiv.org/html/2503.01111v1

- **Harvey, C.R., Ramachandran, A., and Santoro, J. (2021).** *DeFi and the Future of Finance.* Wiley.
  Standard reference for DeFi-RWA architecture.

- **Anonymous (academic, 2025–2026).** *The Tokenisation of Real-World Assets: Financial Innovations, Legal Paradigms, and Systemic Convergence.*
  Treats the three strands of this wiki — finance, law, settlement infrastructure — as a single convergent system. Useful anchor for the synthesis section of any wiki-level MOC. [NLM `1acc9ec3`]

### 4.3 Tokenised-bond empirical evidence

- **Belkhiria, S., Abid, E., and Khiari, W. (2026).** "The impact of tokenization on the trading process costs and carbon emission: Empirical study on the ODDO BHF Bond." *Carbon Balance and Management.*
  Documents **41.5 percent reduction** in trading-process costs and 99.975 percent reduction in per-transaction CO₂ post-Ethereum-Merge. Single most useful empirical study on the cost case.
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12924408/

## 5. Law and Regulation

### 5.1 United Kingdom

- **Law Commission of England and Wales (June 2023).** *Digital Assets — Final Report.*
  Recommends that legislation confirm a **third category of personal property** between *things in possession* and *things in action*, capable of accommodating crypto-tokens, NFTs, and tokenised RWA.
  https://lawcom.gov.uk/project/digital-assets/

- **Property (Digital Assets etc) Act 2025 (UK).** Received Royal Assent 2025.
  Statutory enactment of the third-category recommendation. Leaves the courts to define the boundaries of the new category. [NLM `cdd1af03`, `a6705520`]
  https://lawcom.gov.uk/news/the-property-digital-assets-etc-act-2025-has-received-royal-assent/

- **HM Government (UK).** *Property (Digital Assets Etc.) Bill — factsheet.* Plain-English explainer of legislative intent. [NLM `3d5a5d9b`]

- **UK Jurisdiction Taskforce (UKJT).** *Legal Statement on the Status of Cryptoassets and Smart Contracts* (2019) and *Legal Statement on Control of Digital Assets* (2023).
  Non-statutory but judicially influential statements from senior UK practitioners and judges. The 2023 Statement on *control* is the conceptual bridge between common-law possession and the new third category. [NLM `0910c16f`]

- **FCA (2023–2025).** Discussion papers and consultations on the regulation of stablecoins and tokenised funds.

### 5.2 European Union

- **Regulation (EU) 2023/1114 — Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA).** Adopted 2023; in full effect 30 December 2024.
  Harmonised framework for crypto-asset service providers and issuers, including asset-referenced tokens and e-money tokens. Does not cover tokenised securities or tokenised deposits (those remain under MiFID II, the Prospectus Regulation, and the CRR/CRD framework).
  https://www.esma.europa.eu/esmas-activities/digital-finance-and-innovation/markets-crypto-assets-regulation-mica

- **Regulation (EU) 2022/858 — DLT Pilot Regime.** Allows trading and settlement of tokenised shares, bonds and UCITS under targeted exemptions from MiFID II and CSDR. Operational authorisations include 21X AG (3 December 2024) and 360X AG (29 April 2025).
  https://www.esma.europa.eu/sites/default/files/2025-06/ESMA75-117376770-460_Report_on_the_functioning_and_review_of_the_DLTR_-_Art.14.pdf

- **European Commission (April 2026).** "DLT and tokenisation: paving the way for an 'internet of value'."
  https://finance.ec.europa.eu/news/dlt-and-tokenisation-paving-way-internet-value-2026-04-21_en

### 5.3 United States

- **GENIUS Act of 2025 (Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins Act).** Signed into law 18 July 2025.
  First federal stablecoin statute. Requires one-for-one backing in US dollars or low-risk liquid assets. Prohibits stablecoin issuance by any person other than a permitted payment stablecoin issuer. Takes effect at the earlier of 18 months from enactment or 120 days after final implementing regulations.
  https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/1582
  https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/07/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-signs-genius-act-into-law/

- **CLARITY Act and related digital-asset market-structure legislation (2025).** Allocates jurisdiction between the SEC and CFTC.

- **Executive Order on the digital-asset framework (January 2025).** Establishes the federal posture on tokenisation and stablecoins.

- **President's Working Group on Financial Markets (2021).** *Report on Stablecoins.* Foundational US policy document.

- **2022 Amendments to the Uniform Commercial Code — Article 12 (Controllable Electronic Records).**
  The single most important US **private-law** development for tokenised assets. Article 12 creates a new property category — *controllable electronic records* (CERs) — and adapts the secured-transactions, transfer-of-title, and *take-free* rules of the UCC to digital assets. The functional US analogue to the UK third category and the UNIDROIT Principles. [NLM `93d0f6d4`, `ed22f066`]

- **Morgan Lewis.** *Tokenization of Securities.* Practitioner guide to securities-law treatment of tokenised instruments in the US. [NLM `b51c200c`]

### 5.4 Asia-Pacific

- **Singapore — Payment Services Act 2019 (amended).** MAS stablecoin regulatory framework (August 2023).
- **Hong Kong — Stablecoins Ordinance (2025).** Licensing regime for fiat-referenced stablecoin issuers.
- **Japan — Payment Services Act amendments (2023).** Authorises bank-issued stablecoins (tokenised deposits).
- **UAE — VARA (Dubai) and ADGM frameworks.** Lead regimes for tokenised funds in the Gulf.

### 5.5 New Zealand

- **Financial Markets Authority — guidance on cryptoassets and tokenised products.** No bespoke RWA statute; existing securities law applies functionally to most tokenised instruments.
- **Reserve Bank of New Zealand.** Ongoing work programme on the future of money and the case for a digital cash issuance.
- **FATF/DIA AML/CFT framework.** Applies to virtual asset service providers, including tokenisation platforms.

### 5.6 Cross-border private law

- **Goode, R. (writing for UNIDROIT, 2023).** Commentary on the *Principles on Digital Assets and Private Law.*
- **ALI–ELI (American Law Institute and European Law Institute, ongoing).** Principles project on digital-asset transactions.

- **Academic paper.** *Tokenisation of Absolute Rights and Claims: On the Use of Tokens to Represent Property Rights.*
  Civil-law-tradition analysis of whether a token *is* the right or merely *represents* it. The most rigorous treatment of the "direct vs indirect tokenisation" distinction that drives most of the cross-jurisdictional confusion. [NLM `5c43636a`]

## 8. Worked Example — Property Rights and Land Registry

- **de Soto, H. (2000).** *The Mystery of Capital.* Basic Books.
  The foundational text on why formal property registration unlocks capital. Required theoretical grounding for the property-rights strand.

- **Republic of Georgia + Bitfury Exonum (2016–2018).** Pioneer land-registry blockchain anchoring. Established the *anchoring* model: legal title remains in the state registry; hash proofs are recorded on a public chain for audit.

### 10.4 Agent–blockchain academic spine (added from NotebookLM)

The NotebookLM notebook centres on this strand. Its anchor thesis: there is a structural tension between the **stochastic, adaptive reasoning** of LLM agents and the **deterministic, immutable** state machine of a distributed ledger, and the architectural problem of RWA-on-agentic-rails is the engineering of that interface.

- **arXiv 2601.04583.** *Autonomous Agents on Blockchains: Standards, Execution Models, and Trust Boundaries.*
  The notebook's core paper. Proposes a **five-part taxonomy** of agent capability — from passive read-only analytics through co-pilot, autonomous executor, multi-agent co-ordinator, to fully sovereign on-chain entity — and maps the cumulative authority risks at each stage: prompt injection, key exfiltration, **MEV** (maximal extractable value) exploitation. Advocates **security-first architecture**: account abstraction, multi-party computation, programmable policy engines, and a **Transaction Intent Schema** for auditable agent action. [NLM `a5a899b0`, `036e68b3`, `b9a056d1`, `77fd9062`]

- **(arXiv).** *The Agent Economy: A Blockchain-Based Foundation for Autonomous AI Agents.*
  Articulates the macro-economic counterpart of the engineering paper above. Frames blockchain settlement as the **trust substrate** that makes inter-agent commerce possible at scale. [NLM `c904c9cf`]

- **Emergent Mind.** *Agent–Blockchain Interoperability.*
  Survey-style synthesis of the design space connecting LLM agents to smart-contract execution layers. [NLM `f29e5e48`]
