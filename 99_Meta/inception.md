# wiki-tokenise — Vault Inception Note

## Scope

This vault collates authoritative sources on the **tokenisation of real-world assets** (RWA). It is positioned at the intersection of finance, law, and three worked examples: carbon credits, real estate, and property rights.

A sub-topic strand traces the correlation between RWA tokenisation, payment stablecoins, agentic LLM trading, and the underlying blockchain settlement layer.

## Source categories collated

1. **International agencies and standard-setters** — BIS, IMF, FSB, IOSCO, OECD, World Bank, WEF, UNIDROIT.
2. **Central banks and supervisors** — ECB, Federal Reserve, Bank of England, MAS, HKMA, Banque de France.
3. **Commercial banks** — JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, BNY Mellon, Citi, HSBC, Standard Chartered, Deutsche Bank.
4. **Academia** — peer-reviewed papers and university research centres.
5. **Think tanks** — Atlantic Council, Brookings, Carnegie, RMI, Peterson Institute.
6. **Private sector and industry initiatives** — BlackRock, Franklin Templeton, Securitize, Chainlink, Ondo, Centrifuge, RealT, Toucan, KlimaDAO.
7. **Law and regulation** — UNIDROIT, UK Law Commission, EU MiCA, EU DLT Pilot, US GENIUS Act, NZ FMA position, Article 6 Paris Agreement.

## Method

Sources were identified through:

- Domain knowledge of the field at 2026-05-24.
- Targeted web searches verifying the most recent authoritative outputs (BIS Project Agorá, MAS Project Guardian, HKMA Project Ensemble, US GENIUS Act 2025, UK Property (Digital Assets etc) Act 2025, BlackRock BUIDL AUM).
- Folded in the existing in-vault research report on carbon credit tokenisation (2 April 2026), which contributes ten peer-reviewed papers and three institutional reports.

The drafting standard applied is NZ English. Source descriptions follow scholarly conventions: each entry states what the source argues, the evidence it offers, and its load-bearing relevance to the wiki.
